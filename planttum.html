Planttum

Embedded · ECE 198 · 2023

C STM32 Embedded
  • Built a portable plant monitor on an STM32 Nucleo board in C, reading soil moisture, ambient humidity, temperature, and light intensity in real time
  • Wired a DHT22 temperature/humidity module, photoresistor, and capacitive soil moisture sensor to the board's ADC and GPIO pins, then streamed the live readings to an LCD
  • Presented the technical proposal and design document to the course instructor and received a perfect grade

What it does

Planttum gives a quick read on whether a plant is in a good environment. The board polls each sensor on a loop and surfaces the current readings on the LCD, so you can decide at a glance whether to:

  • water it — soil moisture is below the target threshold
  • move it somewhere warmer or cooler — ambient temperature is outside the plant's preferred range
  • shift it toward (or away from) a window — the photoresistor reports too little or too much light

How it's built

  • Connect the STM32 Nucleo board to a host machine via USB for flashing
  • Wire each sensor to its assigned ADC/GPIO pin on the Nucleo (DHT22 over a single-wire data line, photoresistor and soil probe on analog inputs)
  • Flash the firmware to the STM32 — the main loop samples each sensor, applies calibration, and pushes formatted values to the LCD driver

How to use it

  • Power the board over USB or an external 5V supply
  • Insert the soil moisture probe into the pot — the firmware converts the analog resistance reading into a moisture percentage
  • The DHT22 streams ambient temperature and humidity; the photoresistor reports a relative brightness value
  • All four readings update live on the LCD with no input required from the user
